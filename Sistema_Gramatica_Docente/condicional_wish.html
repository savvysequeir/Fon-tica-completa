II. Explicación

2.1 Wish + Past Simple

Sujeto + wish/wishes + sujeto + pasado simple.

Se usa para desear que una situación presente fuera diferente.

I wish I lived near the university. / She wishes she were more confident.

2.2 Wish + Would + Verb

Sujeto + wish/wishes + sujeto + would + verbo base.

Se usa para pedir un cambio, quejarse de una conducta o desear que algo ocurra.

I wish you would listen. / He wishes they would stop talking.

2.3 Wish + Past Perfect

Sujeto + wish/wishes + sujeto + had + participio pasado.

Se usa para expresar arrepentimientos sobre algo que ya pasó.

They wish they had studied. / I wish I had not said that.

2.4 Negativa

I wish I did not live so far.
She wishes he would not shout.
We wish we had not arrived late.

2.5 Interrogativa

Con wish, la pregunta normalmente se hace antes de la estructura:

Do you wish you lived closer? / Does she wish he would call? / Do they wish they had practiced?

2.6 Regla fina

Con el verbo be, en deseos irreales se prefiere were para todas las personas: I wish I were..., She wishes she were...
