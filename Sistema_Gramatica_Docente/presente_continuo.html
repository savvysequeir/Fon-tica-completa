II. Explicación

2.1 Estructuras

Positiva: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing + complement.
Negativa: Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing + complement.
Interrogativa: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing + complement?
Con modales: Subject + modal + be + verb-ing + complement.

2.2 Positiva: verb to be + -ing

El presente continuo se forma con el verbo to be como auxiliar y el verbo principal con -ing.

  • I am studying.
  • He/She/It is studying.
  • You/We/They are studying.

Reglas para añadir -ing

  • Regla general: work → working.
  • Verbos terminados en -e: se elimina la e. make → making.
  • Verbos terminados en -ie: cambia ie por y. lie → lying.
  • Una sílaba CVC: se duplica la última consonante. run → running.
  • Verbos terminados en -y: se conserva la y. play → playing.

Verbos que normalmente no se usan con -ing

Algunos verbos expresan estados mentales, posesión, sentidos u opiniones. Por eso, normalmente se usan en presente simple, no en continuo.

  • Mente: know, understand, believe, remember, forget.
  • Deseo/gusto: want, need, prefer, like, love, hate.
  • Posesión: have, own, belong.
  • Percepción: see, hear, smell, taste.
  • Estado/apariencia: seem, appear, cost, mean.

Nota: algunos pueden usarse con -ing cuando cambian de significado: I am thinking about you, She is having lunch.

2.3 Negativa

Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing + complement.
  • I am not working today.
  • She is not studying now.
  • They are not playing soccer.

2.4 Interrogative

Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing + complement?
  • Are you listening?
  • Is he cooking dinner?
  • Am I speaking clearly?

2.5 Usos

  • Acciones que ocurren ahora: She is reading now.
  • Acciones temporales: I am working on a project this week.
  • Cambios o procesos: The city is growing.
  • Planes futuros ya organizados: We are traveling tomorrow.
  • Situaciones con modales: He must be waiting.

2.6 Ejemplos

  • She is calling her mother.
  • They are not swimming in the pool.
  • Is he fixing his computer?
  • You must be adding too much salt.
  • She may be visiting her friends.
  • Can you be holding the bag?
