II. Explicación

2.1 Estructura

Sujeto + will / shall + have + been + verbo -ing + complemento

Will funciona con todos los sujetos. Shall se usa sobre todo con I y we en estilo formal.

2.2 Positiva

She will have been studying for two hours.

Reglas de -ing: make → making; run → running; die → dying; play → playing; get → getting.

2.3 Negativa

They will not have been working.

La negación se coloca después de will/shall: will not / shall not + have been + -ing.

2.4 Interrogativa

Will you have been practicing?

La pregunta inicia con Will/Shall, luego sujeto, luego have been y el verbo en -ing.

2.5 Usos

Se usa para expresar una acción que habrá estado ocurriendo durante cierto tiempo hasta un momento futuro: By 8 p.m., I will have been working for ten hours.

2.6 Ejemplos

I shall have been reading.She will not have been sleeping.Will they have been working?
