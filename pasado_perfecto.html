II. Explicación

2.1 Estructura general

El pasado perfecto se forma con had + participio pasado. El auxiliar had se usa con todos los sujetos: I, you, he, she, it, we y they.

Subject + had + past participle + complement.

2.2 Positiva

Se coloca had después del sujeto y luego el verbo en participio pasado. Los verbos regulares usan -ed; los irregulares usan su tercera columna.

She had finished the task.
They had arrived early.

2.3 Negativa

Se agrega not después de had. También puede contraerse como hadn't.

I had not seen that movie.
He hadn't completed the report.

2.4 Interrogativa

Para hacer preguntas, had pasa al inicio.

Had you eaten before class?
Had she chosen the answer?

2.5 Usos

  • Acción anterior a otra acción pasada: She had left before I arrived.
  • Explicar causas pasadas: He was tired because he had worked all day.
  • Mostrar secuencia clara en el pasado: By the time we arrived, they had started.
  • Hablar de experiencias anteriores a un momento pasado.

2.6 Ejemplos y marcadores

Before: antes. After: después. Already: ya. By the time: para cuando. When: cuando.

I had studied before the exam.
They had already left.
By the time he called, she had gone.

Regla clave

El pasado perfecto no usa was / were como auxiliar principal ni usa -ing. Su fórmula central es: had + participio pasado.
