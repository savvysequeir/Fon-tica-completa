II. Explicación

2.1 Estructuras

Positiva: Subject + was/were + verb-ing + complement.
Negativa: Subject + was/were + not + verb-ing + complement.
Interrogativa: Was/Were + subject + verb-ing + complement?
Con modales: Subject + could/might/should/would + be + verb-ing + complement.

2.2 Positiva: was/were + -ing

El pasado continuo expresa una acción que estaba en progreso en un momento del pasado. Se forma con was/were y el verbo principal con -ing.

  • I / He / She / It was studying.
  • You / We / They were studying.
  • Con modales: could / might / should / would + be + verb-ing.

Reglas para añadir -ing

  • Regla general: work → working.
  • Verbos terminados en -e: se elimina la e. close → closing.
  • Verbos terminados en -ie: cambia ie por y. lie → lying.
  • Una sílaba CVC: se duplica la última consonante. run → running.
  • Verbos terminados en -y: se conserva la y. study → studying.

Verbos que normalmente no se usan con -ing

Algunos verbos expresan estados mentales, posesión, sentidos u opiniones. Por eso, normalmente se usan en formas simples, no continuas.

  • Mente: know, understand, believe, remember, forget.
  • Deseo/gusto: want, need, prefer, like, love, hate.
  • Posesión: have, own, belong.
  • Percepción: see, hear, smell, taste.
  • Estado/apariencia: seem, appear, cost, mean.

Nota: algunos pueden usarse con -ing cuando cambian de significado: I was thinking about you, She was having lunch.

2.3 Negativa

Subject + was/were + not + verb-ing + complement.
  • I was not working yesterday.
  • She was not checking her email.
  • They were not achieving their goals.

2.4 Interrogative

Was/Were + subject + verb-ing + complement?
  • Was he acting properly?
  • Were you avoiding the traffic?
  • Were they discovering the truth?

2.5 Usos

  • Acción en progreso en un momento del pasado: She was answering the questions.
  • Acción interrumpida por otra: We were chatting when he called.
  • Dos acciones simultáneas en el pasado: They were dancing while we were talking.
  • Contexto o fondo narrativo: It was raining and people were running.
  • Situaciones con modales continuos: He could be appearing on TV.

2.6 Ejemplos

  • She was accepting the job.
  • They were not collecting items.
  • Was he acting properly?
  • I could be adding more salt.
  • She might not be admiring the view.
  • Would you be developing a plan?
